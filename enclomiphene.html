Research context

Enclomiphene is the trans geometric isomer of clomiphene and is classified as a selective estrogen receptor modulator (SERM). Public databases list free-base CAS 15690-57-0 (formula C26H28ClNO, MW ≈ 406 g/mol) and citrate salt CAS 7599-79-3.

Literature and biochemical reference sources discuss enclomiphene in the context of estrogen-receptor binding studies and endocrine-axis models. Stacked Biologics provides this compound for laboratory use—receptor assays, analytical standards, and other experimental work consistent with institutional policy.

The research format listed here is a solution vehicle (propylene glycol) suitable for laboratory dilution protocols. Vehicle choice for a given assay is the investigator’s responsibility; lot COAs should be reviewed before experimental use.

Laboratory handling notes

  • Store sealed at cool ambient or refrigerated temperature as specified on lot documentation; protect from light.
  • Use appropriate PPE when handling organic research solutions; avoid skin contact and inhalation of aerosols.
  • Propylene glycol vehicle is a laboratory carrier only.
  • Dispose of waste according to institutional hazardous-waste procedures for organic solvents and research actives.
