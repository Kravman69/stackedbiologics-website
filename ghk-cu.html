Research context

GHK-Cu is the copper(II) complex of the tripeptide glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine (GHK). Public compound sources commonly list the free peptide / ligand under CAS 49557-75-7 and the copper complex under CAS 89030-95-5. Molecular weight of the free peptide is approximately 340 g/mol; the copper complex is typically reported near 402–404 g/mol depending on the exact formula used by the registry.

Scientific literature discusses GHK-Cu in the context of copper coordination chemistry, peptide–metal interactions, and experimental models of extracellular-matrix and cell-culture biology. Stacked Biologics supplies GHK-Cu for laboratory investigation.

Although GHK-Cu appears in cosmetic-ingredient databases under names such as copper tripeptide-1, materials on this site are laboratory stocks. Aqueous vehicle and concentration figures describe laboratory material composition only.

Laboratory handling notes

  • Store sealed, cool, and protected from light to limit copper-complex degradation and microbial contamination of aqueous research stocks.
  • Use clean laboratory technique when aliquoting; copper–peptide solutions may stain materials—use appropriate labware.
  • Concentration labels (e.g. percent w/v) describe stock composition for experimental dilution.
  • Follow institutional chemical hygiene and copper-containing waste disposal practices.
