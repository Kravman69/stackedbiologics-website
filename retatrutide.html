Research context

Retatrutide is an investigational peptide developed for research into simultaneous modulation of the glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1), glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP), and glucagon receptors. Public chemical records (including PubChem and related registries) list CAS 2381089-83-2 and synonyms including LY-3437943.

In the peer-reviewed and clinical-development literature, retatrutide is discussed in the context of metabolic pathway research and multi-agonist peptide design. Stacked Biologics supplies this compound solely as a research material for qualified laboratory investigation—assay development, analytical reference, and non-clinical experimental systems as permitted by local law and institutional policy.

Molecular formula and mass figures vary slightly across salt forms and computation methods; investigators should confirm lot-specific certificates of analysis against their experimental requirements.

Laboratory handling notes

  • Treat as a lyophilized peptide solid: minimize moisture exposure; use dry labware when weighing or reconstituting for research assays.
  • Recommended storage: ≤ −20 °C in a sealed container, protected from light, unless lot documentation specifies otherwise.
  • Reconstitution solvents and concentrations should be chosen for the specific in vitro or analytical method; this site does not provide human administration protocols.
  • Follow institutional chemical hygiene plans, PPE requirements, and waste disposal rules for peptide research materials.
