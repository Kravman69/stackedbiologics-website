Research context

Retatrutide is an investigational peptide developed for research into simultaneous modulation of the glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1), glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP), and glucagon receptors. Public chemical records (including PubChem and related registries) list CAS 2381089-83-2 and synonyms including LY-3437943.

In the peer-reviewed and clinical-development literature, retatrutide is discussed in the context of metabolic pathway research and multi-agonist peptide design. Stacked Biologics supplies this compound as a research material for laboratory investigation—assay development, analytical reference, and experimental systems consistent with institutional policy.

Public PubChem records for retatrutide / related “Triple G” entries list molecular formula C221H342N46O68 and a computed molecular weight of approximately 4731 g/mol. Investigators should still confirm lot-specific certificates of analysis (including any salt form) against experimental requirements.

Laboratory handling notes

  • Treat as a lyophilized peptide solid: minimize moisture exposure; use dry labware when weighing or reconstituting for research assays.
