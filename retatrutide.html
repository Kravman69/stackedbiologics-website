Research context

GLP-3 RT40 is an investigational peptide developed for research into simultaneous modulation of the glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1), glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP), and glucagon receptors. Public chemical records (including PubChem and related registries) list CAS 2381089-83-2 and synonyms including LY-3437943.

In the peer-reviewed and clinical-development literature, GLP-3 RT40 is discussed in the context of metabolic-pathway studies and multi-agonist peptide design. Stacked Biologics supplies this compound as a research material for laboratory investigation—assay development, analytical reference, and experimental systems consistent with institutional policy.

Public PubChem records for GLP-3 RT40 / related “Triple G” entries list molecular formula C221H342N46O68 and a computed molecular weight of approximately 4731 g/mol. Investigators should still confirm lot-specific certificates of analysis (including any salt form) against experimental requirements.

Laboratory handling notes

  • Treat as a lyophilized peptide solid: minimize moisture exposure; use dry labware when weighing or reconstituting for research assays.
  • Recommended storage: ≤ −20 °C in a sealed container, protected from light, unless lot documentation specifies otherwise.
  • Reconstitution solvents and concentrations should be chosen for the specific in vitro or analytical method.
  • Follow institutional chemical hygiene plans, PPE requirements, and waste disposal rules for peptide materials.
