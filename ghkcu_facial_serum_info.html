Research Background & Skin Biology

GHK-Cu (Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine Copper) is a naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide that has been extensively studied in dermatological and cosmetic research for its role in skin remodeling and extracellular matrix (ECM) biology.

In research contexts, GHK-Cu has been investigated for its ability to:

  • Stimulate collagen Types I and III, elastin, and glycosaminoglycan production
  • Promote wound healing and tissue remodeling processes
  • Exhibit antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activity in skin models
  • Support hair follicle health and growth in relevant research models
  • Modulate gene expression related to skin repair and anti-aging pathways
Topical Research Application: 2% concentration is a commonly referenced level in published topical peptide research for facial skin biology studies. Patch testing and controlled application protocols are standard in dermatological research.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does GHK-Cu work in skin research?

GHK-Cu delivers bioavailable copper to cells and has been shown in research to reset gene expression to a healthier state, stimulate ECM production (collagen, elastin, proteoglycans), reduce oxidative stress, and support the skin’s natural repair processes.

What is the amino acid sequence?

Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine (GHK) complexed with Copper (Cu²⁺)

Tripeptide with high affinity for copper ions

Is 2% a researched concentration?

Yes. 2% GHK-Cu has been used in multiple published studies on topical application for skin remodeling, wound healing acceleration, and photoaging research models.

Can it be combined with other research compounds?

In research settings, GHK-Cu is sometimes studied in combination with other actives (e.g., hyaluronic acid, vitamins, or other peptides). Compatibility and stability testing should be performed for specific formulations.
